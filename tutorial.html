Landfill Hunting Tutorial

Hi! This is a research application that helps gather more information about landfills across the United States. Currently, there are 1235 landfills in the country. Through this application, we are trying to identify the total square footage of all landfills in the country as well as gather more information about the areas surrounding landfills.

To begin, you will be given an image taken from Bing maps. This location is taken from the Environmental Protection Agency database which has records about all of the landfills in the country. However, this database doesn't have information about the size of the landfills and often the location of the landfill is not exactly where the coordinates specify.

We need your help to find all of the landfills!!!

Once an image is loaded, use the navigation tools to browse around. Zoom in and out. What we are looking for is a large barren landscape, often brown or green. Landfills also often have visible layers of trash build up. This should help you identify a landfill. Here are some example landfill images:

If you think you've found a landfill, use the Polygon tool and click to make points outlining the landfill. Double click to finish. Once the outline is completed, you will see the total area in square meters as well as in football fields (sometimes it's easier to realize how big landfills are by counting the size in football fields!!!). Use the Edit button to change any of the points and use the Delete to remove any misplaced polygons. You may draw more than one outline if you see more than one landfill.

To finish, go ahead and press the 'Save This Data' button. If you aren't able to find a landfill, go ahead and press 'Skip Image' to move onto the next image.

We've also given you a button to identify how sure you are about the landfill. Landfill hunting is tough business! You may not always find one right away!
